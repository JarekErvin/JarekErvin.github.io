About

Jarek Paul Ervin is a music scholar and writer working at the intersection of musicology, cultural studies, philosophy, and social theory. Broadly, Ervin’s thought investigates the overlapping and often conflicting dynamics of music as aesthetic practice, social force, historical inheritance, and a privileged window into the domain of sensory experience. More generally, his research concentrates on the areas of popular music studies; philosophy of music, especially historical materialism, German Idealism, and critical theory (Lukács, Benjamin, Bloch, and Adorno); and gender and sexuality in music.

Ervin is currently a PhD candidate in University of Virginia’s McIntire Department of Music. His dissertation concentrates on the fraught character of New York’s iconic punk scene during the 1970s. The project focuses on a range of important early punk artists, from well-known artists such as Velvet Underground, the Ramones, and Patti Smith, to less-recognized figures including Jayne County, Mumps, and Tuff Darts. Drawing on a range of archival, critical, and recorded materials, Ervin’s project explores the way the category of punk served as a fragile placeholder for a diverse and polemical musical practice during its early history. Ultimately, this leads to an interrogation of broader issues related to genre theory, gender and sexual identity in rock, and popular music’s troubled internal history.

Recent projects have also addressed the early history of popular music studies, avant-garde musical aesthetics, and the recent resurgence of white supremacist music. Ervin is also actively involved in digital and public platforms for music scholarship. He has written for a number of online forums, and actively maintains a blog. Ervin is currently the Assistant Web Editor of the International Association for the Study of Popular Music’s U.S. Branch; follow IASPM-US’s twitter at @IASPMUS.

Ervin’s published work appears with or is forthcoming in Popular Music, Popular music and Society, and Jacobin. He has also presented at a number of recent conferences including 2014’s Left Forum and IASPM-US 2016; this fall, he will present at the joint meeting of AMS and SMT in Vancouver. Ervin has held fellowships from the Library of Congress and UVa’s Vice President's Office for Research, and in 2015 was the Gladys Krieble Delmas Visiting Scholar at the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame’s Library and Archives. In 2016, his work will be supported by a Battestin Fellowship from the Bibliographical Society of the University of Virginia.
